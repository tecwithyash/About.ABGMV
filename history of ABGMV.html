History of ABGMV.

Akhil Bhartiya Gandarva Mahavidyalay (ABGMV) has started in 1901.
It has started by Pandit V.D Paluskar

Early Beginnings (1901)

Pandit Vishnu Digambar Paluskar established the first Gandharva Vidyalaya (school) in Lahore on May 5th, 1901.

Shift to Bombay and Challenges (1907-1924)

In 1907, Paluskar moved to Bombay and set up another Gandharva Vidyalaya in Girgaon the following year.
Despite initial success, the school faced financial and operational difficulties, leading to its closure in 1924.

Carrying on Paluskar's Legacy (1931)

After Paluskar's passing in 1931, his disciples decided to continue his mission of promoting music education.
A meeting was held in Ahmedabad that year, where the Akhil Bharatiya Gandharva Mahavidyalaya Mandal (ABGMVM) was formally established.

Establishment of Affiliated Schools (1930s onwards)

Paluskar's students set up music schools, called Gandharva Mahavidyalayas, across India.
These schools followed the curriculum developed by ABGMVM and students appeared for exams conducted by the board.
The number of affiliated schools has steadily grown over the years.

ABGMVM Today

Today, ABGMVM is a leading organization for Hindustani classical music education in India.
It has over 1200 affiliated institutions and conducts examinations for around 100,000 students annually.
ABGMV played a crucial role in making Hindustani classical music education more accessible to the middle class in India.
It continues to be a significant institution in preserving and promoting this rich musical tradition.
