Biography of Pandit V.D Paluskar.

Here's a glimpse into his life and achievements:

Pandint V.D Paluskar Was born on (10th of August 1870).

Musical Lineage:


His family's original surname was Gadgil,
they came to be known as Paluskar after their village of origin, Palus.
His father was a court musician, exposing Vishnu to music from a young age.

Early Life and Setback


A fireworks accident in his youth left him partially blind.
This, however, turned his focus towards music, where he excelled under the tutelage of renowned Gwalior gharana maestro Pandit Balkrishnabua Ichalkaranjikar.

Champion of Hindustani Classical Music:

Paluskar is credited with reviving Hindustani classical music during a time of decline. He established the Gandharva Mahavidyalaya in 1901, a landmark institution offering formal training in classical music, open to all and not dependent on royal patronage.

Legacy:

He is believed to have popularized the bhajan "Raghupati Raghava Raja Ram" (in a version different from the one sung by Gandhi).
He is also credited with arranging India's national song, "Vande Mataram," in the form it is sung today.
His efforts brought a new level of respect to the profession of musicians in India.
Paluskar's dedication and innovations played a pivotal role in shaping Hindustani classical music as we know it today.
