Introduction of ABGMV.

The Akhil Bharatiya Gandharva Mahavidyalaya Mandal (ABGMVM) is an Indian institution dedicated to promoting and preserving Indian classical music and dance.

Literally translating to "All India Music University Board", the ABGMVM offers courses in

  • Hindustani classical music,
  • Carnatic music,
  • Indian classical dance forms
like Kathak, Bharatanatyam, and Odissi. Their curriculum ranges from beginner level (Prarambhik) to doctorate level (Sangeet Acharya).

The ABGMVM has over 1200 affiliated institutions across India and the world, and conducts examinations twice a year based on their set syllabi.
These exams are offered at over 800 exam centers around the globe.

The ABGMVM was established in 1931

and is considered the successor to the Gandharva Mahavidyalaya, a music school founded in 1901 by Vishnu Digambar Paluskar, a key figure in the spread of Indian classical music education in the early 20th century.
